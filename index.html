Track 13 • Grammar

Present Perfect

We use the Present Perfect to talk about Life Experiences. We don't care *when* it happened, we just want to know *if* you did it in your life.

Birth "Have you ever seen the rain?" NOW

"Ever" means anytime during this entire continuous period.

Past "Did you see the rain yesterday?" NOW
Yesterday

Simple Past focuses on a finished, specific dot in the timeline.

Track 14 • Grammar Structure

The Past Participle

To form the Present Perfect, you MUST use the 3rd form of the verb (V3).

Subject + Have/Has + V3

V1 (Present) V2 (Past) V3 (Participle)
See Saw Seen
Do Did Done
Be Was/Were Been
Know Knew Known
